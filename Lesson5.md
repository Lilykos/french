## 1. Rules for Counting in French

Understanding how to count in French is essential for everyday situations like shopping, telling time, discussing dates, and more. The French number system has some unique features that differ from English, particularly for larger numbers.

### Numbers 1 - 20

Numbers from 1 to 20 are unique forms that need to be memorized individually. The first 16 numbers have distinct forms, while 17-19 follow a pattern based on 10 + units.

| Number | French    | Pronunciation Guide |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| **1**  | un/une    | [uh/yn]            |
| **2**  | deux      | [duh]               |
| **3**  | trois     | [twah]              |
| **4**  | quatre    | [katr]              |
| **5**  | cinq      | [sank]              |
| **6**  | six       | [sees]              |
| **7**  | sept      | [set]               |
| **8**  | huit      | [weet]              |
| **9**  | neuf      | [nuf]               |
| **10** | dix       | [dees]              |
| **11** | onze      | [onz]               |
| **12** | douze     | [dooz]              |
| **13** | treize    | [trez]              |
| **14** | quatorze  | [kah-torz]          |
| **15** | quinze    | [kanz]              |
| **16** | seize     | [sez]               |
| **17** | dix-sept  | [dee-set]           |
| **18** | dix-huit  | [dee-weet]          |
| **19** | dix-neuf  | [dee-nuf]           |
| **20** | vingt     | [van]               |

### Tens (20-100)

After 20, French numbers begin to follow more regular patterns, with some exceptions.

| Number | French    | Notes                                       |
|--------|-----------|---------------------------------------------|
| **20** | vingt     | Basic form                                  |
| **30** | trente    | Basic form                                  |
| **40** | quarante  | Basic form                                  |
| **50** | cinquante | Basic form                                  |
| **60** | soixante  | Basic form                                  |

For numbers between the tens, simply add the unit after the ten:
- **22**: vingt-deux
- **35**: trente-cinq
- **48**: quarante-huit
- **56**: cinquante-six
- **69**: soixante-neuf

**Special Case**: Numbers ending in 1 (except 81 and 91) use "et un" (and one):
- **21**: vingt **et** un
- **31**: trente **et** un
- **41**: quarante **et** un
- **51**: cinquante **et** un
- **61**: soixante **et** un

### Numbers 70-100: Standard French vs. Swiss French

French numbers become more complex from 70-99, with regional variations worth understanding.

#### Standard French (used in France, parts of Belgium, etc.)

| Number | French          | Construction                 |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------------|
| **70** | soixante-dix    | 60 + 10                      |
| **71** | soixante et onze| 60 + 11                      |
| **72** | soixante-douze  | 60 + 12                      |
| **80** | quatre-vingts   | 4 × 20                       |
| **81** | quatre-vingt-un | 4 × 20 + 1                   |
| **90** | quatre-vingt-dix| 4 × 20 + 10                  |
| **99** | quatre-vingt-dix-neuf | 4 × 20 + 19            |

#### Swiss French (used in Switzerland)

In Swiss French (and also in Belgian French), the number system is more logical:

| Number | Swiss French | Construction |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **70** | septante    | Direct form for 70 |
| **71** | septante et un | 70 + 1 |
| **80** | huitante (or quatre-vingts in some regions) | Direct form for 80 |
| **90** | nonante     | Direct form for 90 |
| **99** | nonante-neuf | 90 + 9 |

### Hundreds and Thousands

For hundreds and thousands, French follows these patterns:

| Number | French      | Notes                          |
|--------|-------------|--------------------------------|
| **100**| cent        | Basic form                     |
| **101**| cent un     | Note: no "et" with cent       |
| **200**| deux cents  | Note the plural "s" on "cents" |
| **300**| trois cents | Same plural pattern           |
| **1000**| mille      | Never takes a plural "s"      |
| **2000**| deux mille | No "s" added to mille        |

When combining with other numbers, the plural "s" on "cent" is dropped:
- **205**: deux cent cinq (not "deux cents cinq")
- **350**: trois cent cinquante (not "trois cents cinquante")

### Complex Numbers

For complex numbers combining hundreds, tens, and units:

- **732**: sept cent trente-deux
- **1990**: mille neuf cent quatre-vingt-dix (or mille neuf cent nonante in Swiss French)
- **2025**: deux mille vingt-cinq
- **1578**: mille cinq cent soixante-dix-huit (or mille cinq cent septante-huit in Swiss French)

### Common Expressions with Numbers

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| **On est en quelle année ?** | What year is it? |
| **On est en 2025.** | It is 2025. |
| **Quel âge as-tu ?** | How old are you? (informal) |
| **Quel âge avez-vous ?** | How old are you? (formal) |
| **J'ai vingt ans.** | I am 20 years old. |
| **J'ai trente-cinq ans.** | I am 35 years old. |
| **Ils ont mille livres.** | They have a thousand books. |
| **Il y a deux cent cinquante personnes.** | There are 250 people. |

## 2. Important Vocabulary & Expressions

Learning useful expressions will help you communicate more effectively in various situations. Here are some important French vocabulary items and phrases organized by theme.

### General Expressions & Common Phrases

| French | English | Usage Notes |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **très court** | very short | Description of time/length |
| **court / courte** | short | Masculine/feminine forms |
| **le week-end est court** | the weekend is short | Example usage in a sentence |
| **malheureusement** | unfortunately | Expressing regret |
| **en ce moment** | at the moment | Referring to the present time |
| **c'est dommage** | it's a shame | Expressing disappointment |
| **c'est vrai** | it's true | Agreeing with a statement |
| **je ne suis pas étudiant, je travaille** | I am not a student, I work | Correcting a misunderstanding |

**Examples:**
- **Malheureusement, je ne peux pas venir ce soir.** → Unfortunately, I cannot come tonight.
- **Qu'est-ce que tu fais en ce moment ?** → What are you doing at the moment?
- **C'est dommage que tu ne puisses pas venir.** → It's a shame that you cannot come.
- **C'est vrai, il fait très chaud aujourd'hui.** → It's true, it's very hot today.
- **Les vacances sont très courtes.** → The holidays are very short.

### Education & Reading

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| **quelle page ?** | which page? |
| **la page** | the page |
| **dis-moi** | tell me |
| **qu'est-ce que c'est ?** | what is this? |
| **c'est / ce n'est pas** | it is / it is not |

**Examples:**
- **À quelle page sommes-nous ?** → Which page are we on?
- **Ouvrez vos livres à la page vingt.** → Open your books to page twenty.
- **Dis-moi ce que tu penses.** → Tell me what you think.
- **Qu'est-ce que c'est ? C'est un dictionnaire.** → What is this? It's a dictionary.
- **Ce n'est pas difficile.** → It's not difficult.

### Nature & Environment

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| **le lac** | the lake |
| **la forêt** | the forest |
| **forestier / forestière** | forest-related |
| **varié / variée** | varied |

**Examples:**
- **Il y a un grand lac près de chez moi.** → There is a big lake near my home.
- **Nous aimons nous promener dans la forêt.** → We like to walk in the forest.
- **La région forestière est magnifique.** → The forested region is magnificent.
- **La Suisse a des paysages très variés.** → Switzerland has very varied landscapes.

### Countries & Travel

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| **le pays / les pays** | the country / the countries |
| **la culture** | culture |
| **la langue** | language |
| **la coutume** | custom |
| **étranger / étrangère** | foreign / foreigner |

**Examples:**
- **J'ai visité plusieurs pays européens.** → I have visited several European countries.
- **Chaque pays a sa propre culture.** → Each country has its own culture.
- **Je parle trois langues.** → I speak three languages.
- **Les coutumes varient d'un pays à l'autre.** → Customs vary from one country to another.
- **Je suis étrangère dans ce pays.** → I am a foreigner in this country.

### Asking Questions & Talking about Quantity

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| **combien de** | how many / how much |
| **au moins** | at least |
| **maintenant** | now |
| **pas de + noun** | no + noun |
| **je n'ai pas de maison** | I don't have a house |

**Examples:**
- **Combien de frères et sœurs as-tu ?** → How many brothers and sisters do you have?
- **Il y a au moins cent personnes ici.** → There are at least a hundred people here.
- **Maintenant, nous pouvons commencer.** → Now, we can begin.
- **Je n'ai pas de voiture.** → I don't have a car.
- **Il n'y a pas de problème.** → There is no problem.

### Time & Punctuality

| French | English |
|--------|---------|
| **tu es à l'heure ?** | are you on time? |
| **parfois** | sometimes |
| **en Suisse, on est à l'heure** | in Switzerland, we are on time |

**Examples:**
- **Tu es à l'heure pour le rendez-vous ?** → Are you on time for the appointment?
- **Je suis parfois en retard.** → I am sometimes late.
- **En Suisse, on est généralement à l'heure.** → In Switzerland, people are generally on time.
- **Le train est toujours à l'heure.** → The train is always on time.

### Expressing Desire & Requests

| French | English | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **vouloir** | to want | Infinitive form |
| **je veux** | I want | More direct |
| **tu veux** | you want | Informal |
| **je voudrais** | I would like | More polite |
| **avec toi** | with you | Informal |
| **tu veux venir avec moi ?** | do you want to come with me? | Informal invitation |

**Conjugation of "vouloir" (present tense):**

| Person | Conjugation |
|--------|-------------|
| **je** | veux |
| **tu** | veux |
| **il/elle/on** | veut |
| **nous** | voulons |
| **vous** | voulez |
| **ils/elles** | veulent |

**Examples:**
- **Je veux un café.** → I want a coffee.
- **Je voudrais un café, s'il vous plaît.** → I would like a coffee, please.
- **Tu veux venir au cinéma avec moi ?** → Do you want to come to the cinema with me?
- **Ils veulent apprendre le français.** → They want to learn French.
- **Nous voulons visiter la Suisse.** → We want to visit Switzerland.

## 3. Key Takeaways

✅ **Master the number patterns in French** - Memorize 1-20, understand the tens patterns, and be aware of regional differences for 70-99.

✅ **Remember the construction rules for larger numbers** - No "et" after "cent," no plural "s" on "mille," and know when to use plural forms with "cents."

✅ **Use the correct structure for asking about age** - "Quel âge as-tu ?" and "J'ai ___ ans" (not "Je suis ___ ans").

✅ **Choose between "je veux" and "je voudrais"** - "Je voudrais" is more polite than "je veux" for making requests.

✅ **Practice numbers in context** - Try counting objects, telling your age, discussing years, and talking about quantities.
