## 1. "Il y a" vs. "C'est" - Key Structures for Description

Understanding the difference between "Il y a" and "C'est" is essential for basic French conversation. These two structures serve different purposes and cannot be used interchangeably.

## 2. Adjective Agreement in French

In French, adjectives must agree in gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural) with the noun they describe. This is a fundamental aspect of French grammar that differs from English.

### Basic Rules for Adjective Agreement

1. **Masculine to Feminine**: Usually add -e (but there are many exceptions)
2. **Singular to Plural**: Usually add -s (both masculine and feminine)
3. **Some adjectives don't change in the feminine form** (e.g., facile, difficile)
4. **Position**: Most adjectives come after the noun in French
